Where the Term “Booze” Comes From

ORIGIN OF THE WORD “COCKTAIL”

There is debate about when the term was first used. According to The London Telegraph , the word is first found in print in a March 20, 1798 satirical newspaper article.

Others point to an April 28, 1803 article from The Farmer’s Cabinet in Vermont, where to drink a cocktail was claimed to be “excellent for the head.”

By 1806 the word was clearly defined as “a stimulating liquor composed of spirits, sugar, water, and bitters.”

One theory links the word to horse racing and adulterated spirits. Another claims it comes from mixing barrel tailings.
